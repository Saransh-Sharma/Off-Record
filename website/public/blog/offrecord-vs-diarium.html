OffRecord AI Journal vs Diarium: Privacy and Features Compared

March 23, 2026 · 5 min read

Diarium is a well-built diary app available across Windows, Android, iOS, and macOS. It offers a traditional calendar-based journaling experience with rich media support. OffRecord AI Journal takes a different path — voice-first input, on-device AI, and a privacy model where your data never leaves your phone. Here's how they compare.

Overview

Diarium started as a Windows diary app and expanded to mobile platforms. Its strength is the classic diary experience: a calendar view where each day gets an entry, support for photos and videos, weather logging, and integration with fitness trackers. It uses a one-time purchase model on some platforms and subscriptions on others.

OffRecord AI Journal is built around voice. You speak your thoughts, and on-device AI transcribes, analyzes mood, detects themes, and builds a personal knowledge graph — all without sending a single byte to any server. It's completely free.

Privacy

Diarium: Syncs your diary entries through cloud services like OneDrive, Google Drive, or Dropbox. This means your journal data passes through third-party servers. Diarium itself doesn't run its own cloud — it relies on whichever storage provider you choose. The data is as private as your cloud account is secure.

OffRecord AI Journal: Your data never touches a server. No cloud accounts, no third-party storage providers, no analytics SDKs. Everything runs on your iPhone. Apple's privacy label confirms "Data Not Collected." For a deeper look at why this matters, see our guide to the best journal apps for privacy.

Bottom line: Diarium gives you a choice of cloud providers. OffRecord AI Journal removes the need to trust any cloud provider at all.

AI and Intelligence

Diarium: No AI features. It's a straightforward diary — you write, it stores. Some users prefer this simplicity.

OffRecord AI Journal: On-device AI powers mood detection, sentiment analysis, emotional trend tracking, and a Friday that learns your communication style and personality. It identifies people, places, and recurring themes across your entries. All processing happens on your iPhone's Neural Engine.

Cross-Platform Support

This is Diarium's clear strength. If you use Windows at work, Android on your phone, and macOS at home, Diarium covers all of them with cloud sync keeping everything in sync. OffRecord AI Journal is iPhone-only. If cross-platform access is a requirement, Diarium has the advantage here.

Pricing

Diarium: Pricing varies by platform. The iOS version is around $7.99 as a one-time purchase. The Windows version costs $14.99. Some features may require additional in-app purchases depending on the platform.

OffRecord AI Journal: Completely free. Every feature — voice journaling, AI mood tracking, Friday, encrypted exports, widgets, themes — is included at no cost. No subscriptions, no in-app purchases.

Who Should Choose What

Choose Diarium if:

  • You need a diary app on Windows, Android, and Apple devices
  • You want rich media support including video and drawings
  • You prefer a traditional calendar-based diary layout
  • You don't need AI features and prefer a simple writing tool

Choose OffRecord AI Journal if:

  • You want to journal by voice instead of typing
  • Privacy is a top priority — no cloud storage involved
  • You want AI-powered mood tracking and insights
  • You want a completely free app with no hidden costs
  • You want a Friday that learns from your entries

The Bottom Line

Diarium is a solid traditional diary app with genuinely useful cross-platform support. OffRecord AI Journal is a different kind of journal — voice-first, AI-powered, and built so your private thoughts stay private by design. If you use multiple operating systems and want a classic diary, Diarium is a good choice. If you want depth, intelligence, and privacy on your iPhone, OffRecord AI Journal delivers all three for free.
