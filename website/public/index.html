how it learns

From voice to Friday, five stages.

Every journal entry flows through the same on-device pipeline. Click any stage to see what happens to your data at each step. (Spoiler: nothing leaves the phone.)

stage 01 · capture

Microphone to raw audio, locally.

AVAudioEngine captures AAC audio at 44.1 kHz directly from the iPhone microphone. The buffer is held in memory and handed to the transcriber — never uploaded, never cached to disk.

the stack

Nine Apple frameworks. Zero vendors.

OffRecord AI Journal is built entirely on Apple's on-device SDKs — no third-party dependencies anywhere in the app. Every capability has a home inside iOS.

full story → email github

“I wrote a diary for twenty years. Then I couldn't anymore. So I stopped writing — and started talking. That's how OffRecord AI Journal was born, and why it runs entirely on your phone instead of someone else's server.”

Every voice-journaling app I could find shipped audio to a cloud for transcription. I didn't want my most private thoughts processed by a third party, and I didn't want to pay a subscription for it either. So I built my own — free, open-source, fully on-device. Friday is the next step: a model of you that lives on your phone, not on somebody's server. Read the full story →
